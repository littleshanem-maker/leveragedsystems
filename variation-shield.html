Built from the other side of the desk

We've spent 15 years as project managers on Tier 1 and Tier 2 infrastructure projects across Australia. Our job was processing variation claims from subcontractors — and deciding which ones got paid.

We saw the same thing every week. Skilled tradespeople doing legitimate extra work. Good claims rejected because the paperwork wasn't there. Not because the work wasn't done — because the evidence wasn't documented properly, or it was buried in a text thread nobody could find three months later.

"Everyone on site knew it was extra" doesn't hold up in a final account meeting. The head contractor knows that. Their commercial team knows that. And by the time you're arguing about it, the leverage is gone.

We built Variation Shield because we saw what was missing from both sides. Subbies don't need more paperwork — they need a system that makes proper documentation faster than skipping it. One that creates the evidence chain a head contractor can't ignore.

Every feature in this product exists because we've seen what happens without it. This isn't software built by developers guessing what construction looks like. It's built by people who've lived it.
